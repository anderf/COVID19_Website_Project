  • What is COVID-19?

COVID-19 is a new type of coronavirus that has not been previously identified in humans. According to World Health Organization (WHO), Coronaviruses (CoV) are a large family of viruses that cause illness ranging from the common cold to more severe diseases such as Middle East Respiratory Syndrome (MERS-CoV) and Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS-CoV). Coronaviruses are transmitted between animals and people.

On 30 January 2020, the WHO Director-General declared the COVID-19 outbreak a public health emergency of international concern. In 11 March 2020 the WHO recognised it as a pandemic because more than 189,000 cases of the disease have been reported in over 160 countries and territories, resulting in more than 7,500 deaths and around 80,000 recoveries.

  • How the COVID-19 is transmitted?

    • The virus is transmitted primarily between people in a similar manner to influenza, via respiratory droplets from coughing, sneezing or exhalation including speaking. It is considered to be most contagious when those infected appear symptomatic, although transmission is still possible if those infected appear asymptomatic.

    The time between exposure and symptom onset is typically five days, but may range from two to fourteen days. Currently, there is no vaccine or specific antiviral treatment; treatment efforts consist of symptom alleviation and supportive therapy.

    • Symptoms:

    Symptoms of COVID-19 are non-specific and those infected may either be asymptomatic or develop flu like symptoms such as:

    • Fever
    • Dry Cough
    • Fatigue
    • Shortness of breath
